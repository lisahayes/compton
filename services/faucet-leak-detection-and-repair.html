Faucet Leak Detection & Repair in Compton, CA

A faucet is a precision valve asked to seal perfectly, thousands of times a year, against the grittiest municipal water in the region.

✆ Call (424) 544-0235

Strip any faucet to its parts and you find the same idea: a machined seal, cartridge, ball, ceramic disc, or old-style washer, pressed against a seat to hold back street pressure. Every operation grinds those surfaces against each other. Now add the abrasive: Compton's municipal supply, pumped from the city's own Central Basin wells and classified by its water department as moderately hard to very hard. Each gallon carries dissolved calcium and magnesium that precipitates wherever water slows, sits, or heats. Inside a faucet, that means scale on the seat, grit in the cartridge, and seals wearing out on the schedule of a much older fixture. The drip is not a personality flaw. It is metallurgy meeting mineralogy.

Reading the drip before opening anything

Where and when a faucet leaks names its failure. A drip from the spout with the handles off means the internal seal, cartridge, washer, or disc, is no longer holding. Weeping around the handle base points at O-rings or the cartridge body. Water at the spout collar on a kitchen pull-down usually means the spray hose or its connection inside the cabinet. And moisture under the sink with the faucet dry above often is not the faucet at all, but the supply connections or the sink's own drain assembly wearing out beside it. Five minutes of observation routinely saves an hour of disassembly, so that is where every visit starts.

Repair the guts or replace the fixture: the honest math

A quality faucet with a worn cartridge deserves the cartridge: manufacturers keep them available, the labor is short, and the fixture runs another decade. A builder-grade faucet from the tract era, scaled inside and pitted at the seat, usually is not worth its second repair, because the parts cost approaches a better fixture and the body itself is failing. We carry common cartridges and stems, quote both paths on the spot, and never sell a replacement where a rebuild honestly serves. When we do replace, supply lines and angle stops get replaced with it, since thirty-year-old stops are the component most likely to fail the week after anyone touches them.

The pressure connection nobody makes

Chronic faucet failure across a whole house is rarely about faucets. Street pressure in parts of Compton runs well above the 60 psi fixtures are built for, and when the regulator at the house inlet drifts or dies, every seal in every fixture starts working overtime. The tell is a pattern: multiple faucets dripping within a season, banging pipes, a relief valve weeping at the water heater. Any faucet call from a home with that pattern gets a gauge reading first, because replacing cartridges under 95 psi is landscaping in a windstorm.

From Esprit condos to farmhouse kitchens

Fixture stock tracks housing age here like everything else. The newer townhomes around Esprit run modern single-handle cartridges and ceramic discs, long-lived but scale-sensitive, and their compact under-sink layouts reward tidy work. Tract-era kitchens across the city still hold original two-handle units on their third set of washers. And the older properties toward Richland Farms turn up wall-mount kitchen faucets and vintage brass that parts catalogs abandoned decades ago, where a sympathetic rebuild or a period-appropriate replacement takes actual judgment. Whatever era your fixtures are, the drip is on the meter every hour it runs. A rebuilt cartridge or a well-chosen replacement usually pays for itself within a billing cycle or two, and either way the 3 a.m. metronome in the kitchen goes silent. Faucet questions from Compton kitchens and baths

How much water does a dripping faucet actually waste?

One drip per second is roughly five gallons a day, around 150 gallons over a billing month, per fixture. Two or three lazy faucets quietly match a small genuine leak, all of it metered.

Why does my new faucet already drip?

Usually debris. Fresh installs can pick up solder beads, thread tape, or scale flakes loosened during the work, and one hard particle across a ceramic disc mimics a worn cartridge. Flushing the cartridge often cures it; if not, warranty parts do.

Are ceramic disc faucets worth the extra cost here?

Generally yes, with a caveat: discs are durable but unforgiving of grit, and this water supplies grit. Pair them with an aerator you actually clean and they outlast anything else. Ignore maintenance and a washer faucet fails more gracefully.

Can you match old wall-mount and vintage faucets?

Often. Several makers still produce rebuild kits and period-style replacements for pre-war fixtures, and we tell you honestly when a unit is past sourcing. What we avoid is bodging modern parts into vintage bodies, which fails fast and ugly.
