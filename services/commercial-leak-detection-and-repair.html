Commercial Leak Detection & Repair in Compton, CA

For a business, a leak is measured in lost hours, not just gallons. We work to both clocks.

✆ Call (424) 544-0235

Drive Long Beach Boulevard or Atlantic Avenue and you pass the whole span of Compton commerce: taquerias and tire shops in buildings older than their signage, chain anchors at Gateway Towne Center, light industrial along the rail corridors, and multifamily buildings threaded between them. Every one of those properties runs plumbing harder than any house does, on longer lines, with more fixtures, under health codes and lease clauses a homeowner never reads. When something leaks, the meter is the smallest cost. The real losses are closed doors, canceled covers, and a tenant's trust.

Built for occupied, operating businesses

Commercial diagnosis has to happen around your hours, not instead of them. We schedule detection for closed periods or slow windows, work sections so the rest of the floor stays open, and lean on methods that need no demolition at all for the locating phase: acoustic surveys, thermal imaging across slabs and ceilings, tracer gas in lines we can isolate. When opening is unavoidable, it happens at a confirmed mark, after hours where the space demands it, with containment that keeps dust off inventory and out of kitchens. The repair plan comes to you in writing with the schedule attached, because your decision involves staff and customers, not just pipe.

The failures commercial buildings grow

Scale changes the pathology. Roof drain and overflow lines run through tenant spaces and leak into someone else's ceiling. Long trunk lines feed banks of fixtures, so a single supply failure shows up as pressure complaints across a whole wing. Restaurant and laundromat plumbing lives under grease, heat, and constant cycling that ages it at double speed. Older buildings along the corridors still carry galvanized and cast iron from mid-century, thinned by decades of the city's mineral-heavy well supply. And beneath it all, aging sewer laterals sized for yesterday's occupancy handle today's volume until a root or a rusted-through bottom ends the arrangement, usually on a weekend.

Documentation your lease and insurer will ask for

Commercial leaks come with paperwork, and we produce it as part of the job: cause-of-loss findings, photographed evidence, moisture readings, camera footage on drain and sewer work, and invoices itemized the way property managers and adjusters need. Where responsibility splits between tenant and landlord, our report states plainly where the failure sits and what system it belongs to, which shortens arguments nobody enjoys. Permits go through the city when scope requires them, and inspections get scheduled so your reopening date holds.

Preventive surveys for buildings that cannot afford surprises

The cheapest commercial leak is the one found on a schedule instead of a Saturday night. We run annual and semiannual leak surveys for owners and managers who prefer known numbers to emergency invoices: full-building pressure isolation after hours, thermal scans of slabs and plumbing walls, camera runs through the sewer laterals that age fastest, and a condition report that ranks findings by urgency and cost. Restaurants and laundromats, whose systems age at double time, get the shortest intervals. The survey pays for itself the first time it catches a trunk line weeping into a wall cavity a month before the drywall would have announced it, and it gives lease negotiations and insurance renewals a documented baseline no estimate can match.

From single storefronts to portfolio accounts

Some clients call us once for a flooded stockroom on the Long Beach Boulevard corridor. Others put us on speed dial for a portfolio of buildings from Atlantic Avenue to the industrial edges near the 91. Both get the same operating rules: 24/7 answer at (424) 544-0235, arrival windows we actually hit, findings before invoices, and repairs engineered for commercial duty rather than patched to survive the week. Downtime is the enemy. Precision is how we fight it, and a written scope with a firm schedule is how you plan around whatever we find.

Questions from Compton business owners

Can you work overnight so we stay open?

Yes. Overnight and off-hours work is standard for restaurants, retail, and medical spaces. Detection often happens quietly during business hours; the noisy phase waits for your doors to close.

Who pays when a leak crosses tenant spaces?

The lease decides, but our report gives everyone the facts: which system failed, where it sits, and what it serves. Clear attribution usually settles responsibility faster than any argument.

Do you handle multifamily buildings?

Routinely, from fourplexes to larger complexes. We coordinate unit access with managers, sequence water shutoffs to minimize tenant impact, and give notice templates your office can send residents.

Our water bill jumped but nothing is visibly wet. Where do we start?

With meter and zone isolation after hours, splitting the building into testable sections. Large properties hide pressurized losses in irrigation, trunk lines, and below slabs; isolation finds the guilty section before any invasive step.
