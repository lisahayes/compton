Drain Leak Detection & Repair in Compton, CA

Your water meter swears the house is fine. The stain on the ceiling knows better.

✆ Call (424) 544-0235

Here is the trap built into every drain leak: the meter cannot see it. Your water meter measures what enters the house under pressure, and a drain line carries water only after it has been measured, on its unpressurized way out. A waste line can crack wide open and the meter test comes back innocent, the bill stays flat, and every pressurized-leak instrument in the truck stays silent. Meanwhile each shower, each sink load, each washing machine cycle sends a few cups of gray water into a wall cavity or a ceiling below. Drain leaks are found by different logic entirely, and knowing that up front is what keeps the search short.

The signature of a waste-side failure

Drain leaks keep a schedule, and the schedule is the fingerprint. A ceiling stain that darkens hours after the kids' baths. A musty smell strongest on laundry day. A cabinet that is dry all week and damp after a dinner party. Match the moisture to the usage and you have usually named the fixture group; what remains is finding the failed joint or crack along its run. The stains themselves mislead, though: water travels framing and drywall seams before showing, so the spot on the ceiling can sit six feet from the leak feeding it. When the evidence lives overhead, the ceiling investigation and the drain testing run as one job.

Fill tests, dye, and the camera that ends arguments

Our drain protocol replaces pressure testing with controlled use. We isolate a fixture group, run measured water through it, and watch with moisture meters and thermal imaging at the suspect zones. Dye separates lookalike sources, which shower drain versus which tub overflow. And the inspection camera settles it from inside the pipe: a cracked hub, a rotted cast iron bottom, a separated joint, each visible on recorded footage with its distance logged and its location marked from the surface. Where a branch line's failure sits close to the building's main, the same footage tells us whether the lateral beyond the foundation has the same disease brewing.

Repairs matched to the material

What we cut out depends on what era put it in. Cast iron branch lines in the city's pre-war and early tract stock rust from the bottom up, and a failed section usually means its neighbors are thin too, so we scope enough pipe to recommend honestly between spot repair and rerun. ABS and PVC from later decades fail at solvent joints and hanger points, clean cuts and rebuilt fittings. Galvanized drain arms, still common under older kitchen sinks, are almost always replaced rather than patched, because threading new pipe into rotten threads is theater. Every repair ends with the same proof it started with: water run through under observation, dry joints on camera or under paper towel.

Slab homes, second stories, and the 90222

Compton's construction split decides where drain leaks do their damage. In the slab-on-grade tracts that dominate the 90222 and its neighbors, branch drains run under concrete, and a leak there undermines soil quietly until a floor low spot or a smell announces it, work that borders on our under-slab practice. In the two-story stock and the pre-war homes with raised floors near the Civic Center, drains run through framing, and failures show as the classic ceiling and wall stains. Different geometry, same discipline: match moisture to usage, prove it with the camera, open once. Drain questions from Compton homes

Why did my plumber's pressure test find nothing?

Because pressure tests interrogate the supply side, and your leak lives on the waste side, which never holds pressure in normal service. It is the most common dead end in leak work, and it means the next step is fill testing and a camera, not a bigger pressure gauge.

Do drain leaks smell before they show?

Often. Gray water in a warm wall cavity announces itself as mustiness within days, well before drywall stains. A localized musty smell that tracks a fixture's use pattern is worth treating as evidence, not imagination.

Can a drain leak under the slab be repaired without breaking all the concrete?

Yes. The camera locates the failure precisely, and the opening is cut over that point alone. Depending on pipe condition, some under-slab drain failures also qualify for trenchless-style rehabilitation instead of excavation.

Is a slow tub drain related to a drain leak?

They can share a cause. A bellied or root-invaded line both drains slowly and stresses joints upstream. If slow drainage and moisture symptoms arrive together, one camera run usually explains both.
