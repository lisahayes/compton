The industry left; the housing stayed, and stayed loved. Today South Gate runs one of the older continuous housing inventories on our routes, twenties through fifties stock at density, which makes it a senior-catalog city nearly wall to wall.

South Gate leak questions

Our South Gate house is from 1928. Is anything original still safe?

Often plenty: the era built well, and sound components deserve respect. What earns scrutiny is the supply metal and the drain hubs, both past design life by decades. An assessment grades what remains, ranked honestly.

Why does our water pressure keep dropping year over year?

On this housing stock, classic galvanized behavior: the pipe rusts closed from within long before it leaks. A pressure test and a look at exposed runs confirm it, and the finding usually starts the replacement conversation on evidence.

Do you handle duplexes with shared plumbing?

Routinely, they are native to these blocks. Readings from both units identify the owning system before any opening, and the findings state it neutrally, which keeps neighbor relations intact along with the walls.
